# Social Engineering Attacks

## What are Social Engineering Attacks?
- Social engineering attacks are techniques where an attacker manipulates human psychology to trick individuals into revealing confidential information or performing actions that compromise security.

- attacker exploit trust and urgency to steal data .
---

### Attacker Purpose
- Credential theft: tricking users into revealing usernames, passwords, or personal information to gain access to accounts  
- Data theft and extortion: collecting sensitive company data, intellectual property, or personal information for sale, ransom, or further attacks  
- Malware installation: manipulating victims into downloading malicious files or clicking links that install malware such as ransomware . 
- System access and spying: manipulating employees to gain access to restricted areas or networks, often to install spyware or gain unauthorized access to systems  

---

### how this work 
```
- attacker sends fake email
- victim clicks link
- fake login page opens
- credentials captured  or malware installed 
```

---

### Phishing
- Phishing is a social engineering attack where an attacker sends fraudulent emails or messages to trick users into revealing sensitive information or downloading malicious content 

---

### Smishing 
- Smishing is a social engineering attack that uses fraudulent text messages to trick people into revealing sensitive information, sending money, or installing malware by impersonating legitimate entities like delivery services, banks, or government agencies.

---

### Spear Phishing 
- Spear phishing is a highly targeted attack where an attacker impersonates a trusted source to trick specific individuals into revealing sensitive information, transferring funds, or downloading malware.
- Unlike phishing, spear phishing messages are crafted for a specific person or organization.

---

### Pretexting
- Pretexting is a social engineering tactic where an attacker creates a fabricated scenario to manipulate a victim into revealing sensitive information or performing unauthorized actions.

---

### Baiting 
- Baiting is a social engineering tactic where an attacker creates a trap, often by offering a reward or provoking curiosity, to lure victims into revealing confidential data or performing unauthorized actions.

---

### Prevention 
- **Verify identities independently:** if an organization asks for sensitive data, contact them through a known and official channel instead of using contact details from the message  
- **Be skeptical of urgency:** attackers create panic or excitement to bypass logical thinking, so slow down and assess the situation  
- **Inspect sender information:** check email addresses, URLs, and spelling for small changes that indicate phishing attempts  
- **Use multi factor authentication (MFA):** enable MFA on important accounts to reduce risk even if credentials are stolen  

---

### Personal and technical security practices

- **Use strong and unique passwords:** use different passwords for each account and consider using a password manager  
- **Avoid oversharing on social media:** limit personal information, as attackers use it to craft targeted attacks  
- **Secure physical devices:** lock your devices when not in use and avoid leaving them unattended in public places
